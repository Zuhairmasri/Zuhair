Studying in the U.S and Union

After the many years of academic dedication and community involvement, I got to see the fruits of my hard work. The time I spent studying for the SAT I test, the American Scholastic Aptitude Test, left me with a highly-respected score of 1430/1600, putting me higher than 95% of test takers globally (as stated on my SAT I report). I also achieved a score of 106/120 on the TOEFL IBT English language test, which is in the 93rd percentile on a global scale. Given the immense effort I put in the last few years, I was able to secure a seat in the 2025 Class of Union College. Union is a very prestigious college among U.S higher education institutes, standing at #44 liberal arts colleges in the nation.

A Support Appeal

Although I was able to secure a spot in one of the U.S best colleges out there, there is still one problem that stands an obstacle against me continuing my academic journey: a financial one. Studying in the U.S proves to be quite costly. Union College has offered me a generous grant of $56,317/year for 4 years, covering up to about 71% of the studying fees there. My family will be able to contribute for my education at Union for up to 12.5%, unfortunately leaving about 16% of the studying costs as a hurdle that could stop my pursuit of higher education at a fine higher education institute such as Union College. It would be unfortunate to have to pass on an offer for studying there. I would be very much humbled by the generosity and help of the respected people whose eyes may read my pleas.
